Book Overview

Layman Algebra isn't just another linear algebra textbook — it's the book that finally makes linear algebra click for everyone. Written in a warm, conversational tone like a patient tutor sitting beside you, this book takes a geometry-first approach: you'll see vectors as directions in space, visualize matrices as space transformers, and understand eigenvalues as the directions that don't bend — all before touching a single formula.

About the Author

Ng Ji Wei is a mathematics educator with years of experience teaching IGCSE Mathematics and Additional Mathematics to students across Malaysia, China, Luxembourg and beyond. He is currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Actuarial Studies. As both an educator and a learner frustrated by how most standard textbooks prioritize rote computation over genuine insight, he wrote Layman Algebra to bridge the gap between abstract formulas and intuitive geometric understanding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need any prior math knowledge to read this book?

No. The book starts from absolute basics and builds up gradually. It is designed for complete beginners and students who are new to linear algebra.

Is this book suitable for university foundation studies?

Yes. Layman Algebra aligns with most foundation-level linear algebra syllabi in Malaysian universities, and also serves as a great supplement for undergraduate courses.
